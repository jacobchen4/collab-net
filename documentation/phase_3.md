# Collaboration Network Analysis, Phase 3: Limitations

Jacob Chen, Adrian Rodriguez Vazquez, Madison Seal, Sahaj Baxi

## Data Limitations

### Single Data Source

One of the main considerations for improvements is extending our data source. As we decided on DBLP being our main data source, we only worked with the information they had stored in their XML dump. Combining their data with auxiliary sources (be it additional conferences or other databases containing information for ICSA/ICSE/ECSA) would have provided better data coverage for our analysis.

## Analytical Limitations

### Limited Analytical Scope

The statistical analyses we performed on our data was only pertinent to the information we had directly available; that is, we only gained insight as to the behavior of coauthorship and authorship in ICSE/ICSA/ECSA over the years and when compared to one another. We had also planned to integrate publication and citation data (using external sources such as Semantic Scholar and OpenAlex) to our graph insights in order to test for correlation between coauthorship and publication citation count, author success, publication topic, etc. This was unfortunately not feasible within the timeline of our project.
